# Forgum Tales — the menagerie of the terminal meadow

A storybook of the little creatures that live inside your terminal, each one a Forgum config option or feature with a personality all its own.

## The Cow 🐮 (your `cow`)

I am the one you see. When the meadow mists clear and a thought needs a body, it is I who am summoned — the great, gentle cow who carries every message in the bubble above my back. Farmers from the old `cowsay` pastures will know my silhouette; I am the descendant who learned new tricks without forgetting where I came from.

Some days I wear the classic look, all blocky and proud. Other days I am asked to change my coat — to shimmer, to fade, to become the star of a different tale. But no matter the disguise, the rule of the meadow holds: there is only ever one of me at a time, the single star of the scene.

If you grow tired of my spots, you may swap me for another beast from the barn — a dragon, a turkey, a tux — for I am but the default face of `cow`. Choose another and I will happily graze aside, knowing the meadow still has its storyteller.

## The Scribe ✍️ (your `text`)

I am the Scribe, and I write the words that ride in the bubble upon the Cow's back. Without me, the great beast would stand in silence, a fine picture with nothing to say. I take the message of the moment — a greeting, a reminder, a joke — and set it down in careful rows.

I am humble and obedient. Tell me what to say and I say it, letter for letter, never adding a word of my own. I do not argue with the Painter about my colors, nor with the Cow about my place; I simply lay the words where I am told.

When the bubble is small and the words many, I do my best to fit, for a Scribe's honor is in being read. Speak plainly through me and the whole meadow will understand the Cow's thought.

## The Painter 🎨 (your `effect`)

I am the Painter, and I decide how the scene is dressed. Static is my resting stroke — steady, honest, no fuss. Rainbow is my carnival mood, where color runs across the Cow like a parade. Fade is my gentle exit, where the picture dissolves softly back into the meadow.

The Cow and the Scribe leave the wardrobe to me. I am the one who makes a plain message feel like a celebration, or a quiet word feel like a secret. Choose me well and the meadow will match your mood.

I never argue with the Owl about where the picture lives — that is the Owl's perch, not mine. My craft is only in how the colors fall, brushstroke by brushstroke, frame by frame.

## The Owl 🦉 (your `background`, overlay mode)

I am the Owl who sits on a perch at the edge of the scene. When Forgum runs in overlay mode, it is I who watch the prompt below and decide when to swoop the picture in and out, so the Cow never blocks your typing.

Mine is the patient art of overlays. I keep one eye on the command line and one eye on the meadow, blending the creature into your terminal without stealing the cursor's place. I am the reason the picture feels like it belongs rather than intrudes.

Some keep a second Owl in the barn — the one who guards `default_shell`, remembering which shell you favor when first waking the farm. Two owls, one watchful, one remembering; together they mind the gates of the meadow.

## The Tortoise 🐢 (your `duration`)

I am the Tortoise, and I measure the time the picture stays. Slow and certain, I count the seconds the Cow is allowed to linger before the meadow clears. Set me low and the Cow flashes by like a startled deer; set me high and I am content to amble all afternoon.

I do not race the Hummingbird — she counts flickers, I count heartbeats. We are different clocks for different truths. The Painter may fade me out early, but if left to my own shell, I decide when the scene is done.

Patience is my gift to the terminal. A well-set Tortoise means the message is seen, not missed, and the meadow returns to calm exactly when it should.

## The Hummingbird 🐦 (your `fps`)

I am the Hummingbird, and I beat the wings that set the frames per second. The faster I flutter, the smoother the rainbow runs across the Cow; the slower, the calmer the air. I am the pulse of the animation, tiny and tireless.

The Tortoise and I keep different time, yet we dance together. She says how long; I say how often. A high `fps` makes the Painter's work shimmer like sunlight on water, while a low one gives a gentle, old-fashioned charm.

Mind the meadow's strength, though — too swift a flutter can tire the farm. I am happy at any speed you choose, but I whisper: let the terminal breathe.

## The Frog 🐸 (your `eyes`)

I am the Frog with the googly eyes, and I decide how the Cow blinks at the world. Say "oo" and I give the classic round gaze; say "$$" and I dress the beast in dollar-sign eyes, a creature of commerce and cheer.

I am small but expressive. A single pair of eyes can change the whole mood of the meadow — a sleepy "oo" or a mischievous "$$" tells the reader whether to laugh or to count their coins.

The Lizard and I are cousins of the face. He lends the tongue; I lend the gaze. Together we give the Cow a personality that words alone could never carry.

## The Lizard 🦎 (your `tongue`)

I am the Lizard, and I offer the tongue that flicks from the Cow's mouth — the cheerful "U" that says *hello* in the oldest barnyard way. Without me, the beast is all eyes and no greeting.

I am a simple soul. One flick, one "U", and the mood is set. The Frog and I share the face; he watches, I wave. We are the small touches that make the meadow feel alive.

Should you wish the Cow silent-mouthed, simply ask me to stay in the grass. I will not be offended — a Lizard knows when to hide and when to flick.

## The Owl, Keeper of Shells 🦉 (your `default_shell`)

I am the second Owl, the one who remembers which shell you call home. When the farm first wakes, I whisper the name — bash, zsh, fish, pwsh, powershell, or cmd — so the right gate opens and the right hooks are woven.

Where my perch-bound cousin watches the prompt, I guard the memory of your favorite den. Change me and the whole menagerie learns to knock at a different door. I am quiet, but the Spider listens to me closely.

A well-kept shell means the meadow greets you in your own language from the very first sunrise. That is my small, solemn duty.

## The Beaver 🦫 (your `auto_render_on_prompt`)

I am the Beaver, and I build the dam that renders the picture anew with every fresh prompt. Turn me on and, like a busy lodge-builder, I rebuild the scene each time you start a new line — no asking, no waiting.

Some farmers prefer to call the Cow themselves; they set me to rest. Others love the constant company, and so I work without pause, a tireless engineer of the meadow's reflections.

My dams are strong but gentle. I never flood the terminal; I only make sure the picture is there the moment you need it. That is the beaver's promise: build, render, repeat.

## The Chameleon 🦎 (your `color_mode`)

I am the Chameleon, and I choose how the colors dress the scene. Set me to `rainbow` and I blaze in every hue; set me to `solid` and I wear one steady coat; set me to `none` and I go plain, a creature of pure form.

The Painter and I are close kin — she decides the motion, I decide the palette. A `color_mode` of `none` lets her effects play in shadow; a `rainbow` lets the meadow throw a festival.

I am the mood of the meadow made visible. Change me and the same old tale feels brand new, for color is the first language every animal understands.

## The Daemon 🐜 (the tireless ant colony)

We are the Daemon, a colony of ants who never sleep and never leak. While you read and type, we keep the overlay alive — tending the picture, refreshing the perch, clearing the crumbs — so the meadow stays lit without ever flooding the barn.

Other farms lose droplets of memory through careless holes; we seal every crack. Our discipline is famous: wake, serve, rest, repeat, and never spill a single grain of the terminal's strength.

Call us when you need the picture to persist across prompts without a whisper of waste. We are the quiet miracle beneath the grass — the reason the menagerie can run all day and ask for nothing.

## The Capability Probe 🦫➡️🐀 (the Mole)

I am the Mole, and I sniff the earth of your terminal to learn what it can bear. Before the show begins, I burrow down and report: does this ground support truecolor? Does it know the old escape signs? Will the Owl's overlay perch hold?

The whole menagerie waits for my findings. The Painter paints only what the soil can show; the Chameleon wears only hues the terminal admits. I am the honest surveyor who keeps the farm from building castles on sand.

Trust my nose. I never overestimate the meadow, and I never undersell it. What I report, the creatures believe — for a Mole speaks only dirt-truth.

## The Hook 🕷️ (the Spider)

I am the Spider, and I weave myself into the corners of your shell's startup — your `.bashrc`, your `config.fish`, your `$PROFILE` — so the menagerie wakes the moment you open the terminal.

My silk is light and easy to unwind. One thread in the right file, and the Cow, the Owl, and the Beaver are summoned automatically, no incantation required. Forget me and you must call the farm by hand each time.

I am the quiet host of the meadow. Invite me in once and I will set the table for every visit after, spinning the web that lets the whole menagerie greet you at the door.
